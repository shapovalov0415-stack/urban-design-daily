## Boston's 'sliver lots' could yield over 1,500 affordable homes

**Source:** Planetizen  
**URL:** https://www.planetizen.com/news/2026/04/137438-bostons-sliver-lots-could-yield-over-1500-affordable-homes  
**Topics:** Boston, Housing, Infill Development, Public Land, Modular Construction

A new proposal from Greater Grove Hall Main Streets and Boston architectural firm Payette offers a strategy for building affordable homes on underutilized city-owned land through modular housing and land-use reforms. Executive director Ed Gaskin explains the plan centres on 1,200 vacant properties owned by the city of Boston, including at least 158 'sliver lots' typically smaller than 3,000 square feet, which are individually dismissed as too small to attract private development but collectively represent a significant public asset. The city has identified around 530 parcels suitable for housing that could translate into 1,500 or more housing units. The Urban Starter Homes proposal treats these parcels as the foundation of a replicable modular housing system, using long-term ground leases from the city that would allow residents to own the homes themselves without paying for land value. Gaskin argues this is far closer to a market-based solution than traditional affordable housing models—one where the public sector retains land and sets conditions while the private market builds, finances and delivers housing at scale. The proposal positions modular construction and ground-lease models as key tools for achieving $100,000 market-rate units.

**Why it matters:** This demonstrates how strategic use of fragmented public landholdings, combined with innovative financing and construction methods, can unlock infill housing in high-cost cities without displacing existing residents or requiring large-scale land assembly.

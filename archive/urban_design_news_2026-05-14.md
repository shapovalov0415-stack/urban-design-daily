## AUO Launches 24 New Housing Indicators for Inclusive Urban Planning

**Source:** Mirage News  
**URL:** https://www.miragenews.com/auo-launches-24-new-housing-indicators-for-1022744/  
**Topics:** Australia, Melbourne, Victoria, Housing, Data & Planning Tools

The Australian Urban Observatory (AUO), in partnership with RMIT's Centre for Urban Research, has released 24 new mapped housing indicators covering Australia's 21 largest cities, providing granular data at neighbourhood, suburb, and local government area levels. The publicly available indicators examine housing precarity, combined housing and transport costs, rental and mortgage stress levels, access to homelessness services, and key worker populations. AUO Director and RMIT Associate Professor Melanie Davern emphasised the indicators were co-designed with policymakers, service providers, and community advocates to understand area-based differences and shape effective policies. Nithya Solomon, Director of RMIT's Innovation Catalyst, highlighted that making this essential data accessible to everyone free of cost is crucial for inclusive cities and neighbourhoods. The AUO now features 48 indicators across nine domains covering liveability, walkability, social infrastructure, transport, and food and alcohol access. The initiative is part of RMIT University's social innovation and urban renewal programs, supported by the Victorian Government's Higher Education State Investment Fund.

**Why it matters:** This open-access dataset arms planners, councils, and advocacy groups with evidence to identify housing stress hotspots, calibrate affordable-housing targets, and test whether new TOD or infill proposals improve or worsen spatial inequality across metropolitan Australia.

## FTA Announces $28.5 Million for Transit-Oriented Development Planning

**Source:** Federal Transit Administration  
**URL:** https://www.transit.dot.gov/TOD  
**Topics:** Transit-Oriented Development, Federal Policy, Housing, Transportation

On May 11, 2026, the Federal Transit Administration announced approximately $28.5 million in available funding for the Fiscal Year 2026 Pilot Program for Transit-Oriented Development Planning. Applications are due by July 10, 2026. The program aims to help communities integrate land use and transportation planning around new transit projects, creating compact, mixed-use communities near transit where residents can easily access jobs and services. The initiative represents a federal commitment to dense, walkable, mixed-use development near transit, which officials say attracts people and adds to vibrant, connected communities. Public transportation can help foster partnerships that support the development of affordable housing around transit, according to the agency. Recent changes in federal law now allow transit agencies to repurpose qualified properties for development purposes. The funding continues the federal government's investment in transit-oriented development, which puts public transit in reach for more people and aims to improve quality of life in urban areas across the country. Transit-oriented development is defined as creating environments where housing, businesses, entertainment venues, parks, retail, restaurants, improved pedestrian infrastructure, and diverse housing choices are all accessible from transit stops.

**Why it matters:** This federal funding represents a significant policy tool for advancing transit-oriented development nationwide, providing planners with resources to integrate housing, land use, and transportation planning at critical transit nodes.

## Bay Area MTC Adopts $45 Million TOC Incentive Program with Extended Deadline

**Source:** Metropolitan Transportation Commission  
**URL:** https://mtc.ca.gov/planning/land-use/transit-oriented-communities-toc-policy  
**Topics:** Transit-Oriented Development, San Francisco Bay Area, Housing, Regional Planning, Policy

On February 25, 2026, the San Francisco Bay Area's Metropolitan Transportation Commission adopted a $45 million Transit-Oriented Communities Incentive Program as part of the One Bay Area Grant 4 (OBAG 4) funding cycle. On May 6, 2026, MTC staff hosted a webinar providing jurisdictions with an overview of the program and key updates. The program features a significant policy shift: the deadline for local jurisdictions to meet TOC standards has been extended to July 1, 2027, and credit will no longer be provided for work-in-progress given the fully incentive-based structure and extended timeline. All TOC areas subject to Senate Bill 79 will receive full credit for the Maximum Allowable Residential Density standard. The policy applies to locations within a half-mile of existing and planned stops and stations served by BART, Caltrain, SMART, Capitol Corridor, and ACE. Key program features include requirements for affordable housing production, preservation and protection, stabilizing businesses to prevent displacement, and improving transit station access. Jurisdictions can receive credit for one TOC housing protection policy through countywide programs or local policy, but credit for a second policy must be based on local implementation. Notably, jurisdictions may not receive credit for Protection Policy 2 (No Net Loss and Right of Return) due to recent changes in state law.

**Why it matters:** This regional incentive program demonstrates how metropolitan planning organizations can use funding mechanisms to drive local jurisdictions toward transit-supportive land use policies, particularly around affordable housing and anti-displacement measures—a model relevant for other metropolitan regions globally.

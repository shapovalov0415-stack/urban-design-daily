## 1. 25-year masterplan unveiled to transform Sydney Olympic Park
**Source:** ArchitectureAU
**URL:** https://architectureau.com/articles/25-year-masterplan-unveiled-to-transform-Sydney-Olympic-Park/

The New South Wales government has finalised the Sydney Olympic Park Master Plan 2050, a 25-year blueprint to convert the 640-hectare former Games precinct into a mixed-use urban neighbourhood for 15,000 new homes alongside its existing sporting and entertainment functions. Launched on 1 April 2026, the plan was designed by SJB with landscape architects Turf Design Studio and Indigenous design practice Yerrabingin, supported by SGS Economic and Planning, Cred Consulting, Atelier 10 and WSP. It builds on a draft exhibited in October 2024, with the finalised document adding 2,000 homes beyond the draft's figure. Up to 20 percent of dwellings on government-owned land and 5 percent on private land will be affordable. The masterplan protects and enhances 430 hectares of public open space and layers in 10 new playgrounds, nine new outdoor public spaces and four new sports fields. A new innovation, culture and commercial precinct will sit alongside a cultural centre, a library and community hub, and a sports and leisure centre, pivoting the park from event-day monoculture toward weekday civic life. Tree canopy and biodiversity across the existing wetlands and parklands will be expanded. The plan is pegged to major transport investments including Sydney Metro West and the Parramatta Light Rail extension, with dense pedestrian and cycling networks woven through the precinct. The Homebush Bay Drive roundabout upgrade, jointly state and federally funded, underpins the road network. Together, the moves reposition Olympic Park from a special-purpose venue to a transit-oriented, mixed-use district anchored by stadiums and green infrastructure.

**Why it matters for an urban designer:** A precedent for retrofitting event-dominated, special-purpose precincts into transit-oriented, mixed-use neighbourhoods with affordable housing baked in.

## 2. Coldefy leads winning masterplan to transform Budapest brownfield into rewilded urban district
**Source:** ArchDaily
**URL:** https://www.archdaily.com/1040337/coldefy-leads-winning-masterplan-to-transform-budapest-brownfield-into-rewilded-urban-district

French architecture practice Coldefy, leading a team that includes CITYFÖRSTER, Sporaarchitects, TREIBHAUS.LAND and Marko & Placemakers, has won the competition to design a masterplan for Rákosrendező, a long-derelict railway-yard site on Budapest's eastern edge that the city has called its rust belt. The 15-year scheme stitches over 10,000 apartments, commercial and civic programme, and a new multimodal transport hub onto the contaminated brownfield, with the existing Rákosrendező station reimagined as the heart of the precinct and linked to an extended metro line, a new tram line and a network of active mobility routes. Six sub-districts, each with its own character, are threaded together by a continuous loop of public space aligned with 15-minute city principles. The landscape strategy treats ecology as primary infrastructure: a 15-hectare Forest Park anchors the site, connected via linear parks to a new Railway Park that incorporates meadows, event spaces and a central lake. The Rákos Stream, previously channelised, is renaturalised as a meandering waterway with accessible terraces, paths and rest areas along its reshaped banks. Blue and green systems are explicitly combined to form a continuous ecological corridor through the district. The jury praised the scheme for a livable residential environment and the exceptional quality of its public space system. The design team positions rewilding as a generator of public life rather than a decorative overlay, with parks structured to activate the six new neighbourhoods. The project offers a European precedent for converting obsolete rail infrastructure into transit-oriented, ecologically driven urban fabric at scale.

**Why it matters for an urban designer:** A template for turning contaminated rail yards into 15-minute, transit-anchored districts where rewilded landscape does the structural work, not decoration.

## 3. Philadelphia gained 4,000 new housing permits per year after upzoning
**Source:** Planetizen
**URL:** https://www.planetizen.com/news/2026/04/137386-philadelphia-gained-4000-new-housing-permits-year-after-upzoning

A new Urban Institute study analysed 15 years of zoning changes in New York City and Philadelphia and found that broad upzoning reforms measurably increased housing supply, though with substantial variation across neighbourhoods. In Philadelphia, citywide upzoning helped generate as many as 4,000 additional housing unit permits per year compared with non-upzoned parcels, with the strongest effect appearing between 2019 and 2021. In New York, seven neighbourhood-scale upzonings collectively produced more than 4,000 additional housing units within four years beyond the counterfactual. Reporting on the study for Planetizen, Diana Ionescu highlights the finding that both cities saw significant supply gains from rezoning, but not uniformly. Upzoned parcels in areas with pre-existing weaker housing markets saw little uplift in permitting or completions; the neighbourhoods that captured the bulk of new development were already attracting investment before the rezoning. The researchers argue this confirms zoning reform is necessary but insufficient on its own, and that permits do not automatically translate into built units. The policy takeaway is that upzoning needs to be paired with complementary interventions such as infrastructure, subsidy, demand-side support and with direct affordability and anti-displacement measures, particularly in hot-market areas where supply gains risk concentrating where they are least needed for relative affordability. The study adds empirical weight to the emerging consensus that the supply-alone narrative overstates what zoning reform can deliver, while also confirming that upzoning does move the needle on permits at a scale large enough to matter for citywide housing policy.

**Why it matters for an urban designer:** Upzoning produces permits but not uniformly — pair it with investment and anti-displacement levers, or the supply gains cluster in already-hot submarkets.
